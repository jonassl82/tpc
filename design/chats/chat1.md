## User

Design a landing page for a B2B talent network called The Platform Collective.

WHAT IT IS
A hand-vetted network of senior, ex-platform paid-social operators (ex-Meta, ex-Snap, ex-TikTok, ex-agency leads). Companies bring a need, we scope it into a defined project, and a vouched-for operator delivers. No retainers, no agency overhead, no junior account managers. Senior paid-social expertise, on demand, project-based.

WHO IT'S FOR
Growth leads, heads of performance, and founders at ecommerce, retail, and consumer brands who spend real money on paid social and are tired of choosing between bloated agencies and unvetted freelancers. They know the outcome they want but not who to trust or how to scope it.

THE CORE PROMISE
Senior operators who have actually run this at scale, matched to your exact project, scoped properly before it starts. You describe the outcome. We define the deliverables, timeline and price. Someone excellent delivers.

PAGE STRUCTURE
1. Hero. A sharp headline landing the wedge (senior ex-platform paid-social talent, scoped and on demand, no agency, no retainer). One-line subhead. One primary CTA: "Describe your project." A quieter secondary link for operators: "Join the network."
2. How it works, three steps: You describe the outcome. We scope it into a defined project. A vetted operator delivers. Make this visual and dead simple.
3. What people bring us: the five project types as cards. Campaign planning and strategy. Measurement and incrementality. Creative planning and testing. Channel and market expansion. Account audits and diagnosis.
4. Who the operators are: a trust section. Ex-Meta, ex-Snap, ex-TikTok, ex-agency leads. Hand-vetted, senior only. Convey credibility without listing fake names, use role and background chips or short credibility statements rather than invented testimonials.
5. Why this over an agency or a freelancer: a short three-column contrast. Us versus agency (no bloat, no retainer, senior only). Us versus freelancer (vetted, scoped, accountable).
6. Final CTA band: "Tell us what you need." Simple, confident.
7. Minimal footer.

TONE AND VOICE
Confident, senior, plain-spoken. This is premium expertise, not a cheap marketplace. No hype, no exclamation marks, no buzzword soup. Short lines. Sounds like an operator who knows their worth, not a startup trying too hard.

VISUAL DIRECTION
Premium and restrained, closer to a boutique consultancy than a SaaS tool. Strong typography doing most of the work. Confident use of whitespace. A tight, serious color palette, not playful. It should feel like a place senior people would be proud to be listed and companies with budget would trust. Avoid generic startup gradients and stock-illustration clichés. Make deliberate type and layout choices.

IMPORTANT
No invented testimonials, client logos, or fake stats. Use honest placeholder credibility (role-based, background-based) that I can fill in with real operators later. Do not put specific pricing on the page. Keep two clear audiences addressed but keep the company the primary one, operators secondary.

Give me a single, polished, responsive landing page.
